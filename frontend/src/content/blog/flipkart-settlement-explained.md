If you sell on Flipkart, you've probably noticed the amount that lands in your bank account is nowhere near the order value the customer paid. That gap is where most sellers lose track of their real profit.

## The order value is not your revenue

When a customer pays ₹599 for your product, Flipkart does **not** transfer ₹599 to you. The settlement amount — the money that actually reaches your bank — is what's left after a stack of deductions.

## The deductions that shrink your payout

A typical Flipkart settlement subtracts several fees before paying you:

- **Commission fee** — a percentage of the order value, varying by category.
- **Shipping fee** — the cost of moving the parcel, often weight-based.
- **Collection fee** — a charge for collecting payment from the customer.
- **Fixed fee** — a flat per-order charge.

By the time these come out, your ₹599 order might settle at ₹430 — before you've even subtracted what the product cost you to buy.

## Why settlements arrive in pieces

Flipkart often pays a single order across **multiple settlement files** over several weeks. The base payment might land first, with promotional reimbursements or fee adjustments following later. If you only look at one settlement file, you'll undercount what you actually earned.

## Real profit is settlement minus cost

Your true profit on an order is the **final settlement amount minus your purchase price** — and minus any reverse-shipping fee if the order was returned. Tracking that by hand across hundreds of orders and multiple settlement files is where spreadsheets fall apart.
