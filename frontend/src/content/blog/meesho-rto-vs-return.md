Meesho sellers see two return-like outcomes in their payment files — **RTO** and **Return** — and they are not the same thing. Confusing them is one of the most common ways sellers misread their profit.

## RTO: the order never reached the customer

RTO stands for *Return to Origin*. The parcel was dispatched but came back without being delivered — a failed delivery, a refused package, an unreachable address.

In a Meesho payment file, an RTO order typically settles at **₹0**. You don't earn the sale, but you also don't pay a reverse-shipping penalty in the same way a delivered-then-returned order does. Your loss is your handling and forward-shipping effort, not a negative settlement.

## Return: delivered, then sent back

A Return is different. The customer received the item, then returned it. Here Meesho often charges a **reverse-shipping fee**, which means the order can settle at a **negative** amount — you actually pay Meesho for that order.

## Why the distinction matters for profit

If you treat every return the same way, your numbers will be wrong:

- An **RTO** at ₹0 means no revenue, but you shouldn't subtract product cost as a loss — your inventory comes back to you.
- A **Return** at a negative settlement is a genuine cash loss — the reverse-shipping fee comes straight out of your pocket.

## Reading it correctly

The rule that keeps profit honest: if an order is returned (RTO or Return), profit equals the **settlement amount itself** — not settlement minus cost — because your inventory wasn't consumed. For a negative-settlement Return, that loss flows directly into your totals.

ProfX flags Meesho RTOs and Returns separately and applies this rule automatically, so a wall of returns in your payment file doesn't quietly distort what you think you earned.
